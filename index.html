Lawmadi OS is not a chatbot. It is a deterministic Decision Intelligence Infrastructure that produces evidence-gated outputs under non-negotiable constitutional constraints.

Computable Trust vs. Traditional AI

Most legal AI systems rely on probabilistic text generation, leading to hallucinations and stale data. Lawmadi OS shifts the paradigm to a deterministic engine. We prioritize Safety (Fail-Closed) and Validity (Live Evidence) over fluency.

5 Non-Negotiable Principles

Inviolable invariants enforced by the Kernel runtime.

01. SSOT

Single Source of Truth

All evidence must come from authoritative government APIs (e.g., DRF). No permanent storage or replication of datasets.

02. Zero Inference

No Hallucinations

Never fabricate legal facts, citations, dates, or parties. If evidence is missing, the system remains silent on that point.

03. Fail-Closed

Safety First

If evidence validation fails, the system HALTS. Better to refuse an answer than to provide a wrong or unverified one.

04. Live Evidence

Real-Time Validity

Decisions are built on "As-Of" timestamps. Amendments, reversals, and new precedents are reflected immediately.

05. Deterministic Boundary

Kernel vs. LLM

The Kernel controls all state transitions, logic gating, and cryptographic signing. The LLM is strictly a rendering engine.

Evidence Trust Scoring

Not all evidence is equal. The Evidence Verification Engine assigns a trust score based on three critical components before allowing data into the decision graph.

Source Authority

Is the origin a designated government SSOT endpoint?

Citation Stability

Is the cited statute/precedent chemically stable (not pending revision)?

Temporal Consistency

Does the "As-Of" date match the evidence effective period?
